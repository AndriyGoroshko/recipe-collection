  1. Cut 100 g of butter into pieces and place in a saucepan with 150 g of honey. Cook, stirring, until the butter melts and combines with the honey. Turn off the heat and add 1 tsp baking soda, 100 g sugar, and ½ tsp salt. Mix well.
  2. Beat 2 eggs and pour them into the honey mixture. Stir and return the mixture to low heat to warm slightly.
  3. Remove the mixture from the heat and add 500 g of flour. Mix the dough—first with a silicone spatula, then finish kneading by hand.
  4. Lightly flour the table, place the dough on it, and divide it into 12–13 pieces of about 70 g each. Keep 2 pieces out to work with and cover the rest with plastic wrap in the fridge, taking them out one at a time.
  5. Preheat the oven to 180°C (without convection). Roll out each dough piece very thinly between two sheets of parchment paper. Remove the top sheet and cut out a circle using a plate or ring about 20–22 cm in diameter. Bake the scraps too—they’ll be used for the crumb topping.
  6. Poke the dough with a fork in several places. Bake each layer for 5 minutes, then remove and let cool. Repeat for all layers.
  7. Mix 800 g of cold sour cream, 100 g of powdered sugar, and 50 g of honey. Whip with a whisk or mixer until smooth and fluffy. If the cream isn't very thick, chill it in the fridge for a while.
  8. Assemble the cake: spread a little cream on the center of the serving plate. Place the first cake layer and spread it with cream. Continue layering and spreading cream until all layers are used.
  9. Crush the baked scraps into crumbs. Cover the top and sides of the cake with cream and sprinkle with the crumbs.
  10. Refrigerate the cake for at least 4 hours—or better, overnight—so the layers soak up the cream well.
